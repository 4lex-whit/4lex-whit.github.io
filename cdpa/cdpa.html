What is the Copyright Design & Patents Act?

The Copyright Design & Patents Act was introduced in 1988, and protects the intellectual property (defined as "intangible creations of the human intellect") of a person or organisation.

Copyright is automatically applied to original pieces of work, and - depending on the type of work - will only expire 25 to 70 years after the owner's death.
