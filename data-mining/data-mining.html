What is data mining?

Data mining is the act of taking meaningless data, and finding patterns and trends in it, turning it into useful information. It's performed on a small sample of the data, and then the patterns and trends found in it are extrapolated and applied to the entirety of the data.

Data mining can be performed on practically any set of data. For example, it could be used on weather data to estimate future weather, shopping and transaction history to estimate what products will sell the best at what times, etc.
